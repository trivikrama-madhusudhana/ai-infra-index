The physical AI race, scored on public evidence

Model benchmarks are everywhere. The megawatts are not. This index tracks the infrastructure the frontier labs actually control, and every number on it traces to a source you can open. Capacity is credited to whoever owns the site, so a lab that rents its compute scores low here on purpose.

How to read this

Each lab gets a score from 0 to 100. It rewards infrastructure a lab actually owns and can point to in public: power its datacenters draw today, sites it holds, chips it designs, deals it has signed. Renting compute from someone else's cloud counts for little. Five things go into the score:

What we measureIn plain termsWeight
Power capacityMegawatts the lab's own datacenters draw today or are actively building.30%
Compute ownershipHow many AI chips it controls, and whether it designs its own.20%
Vertical integrationHow much of the stack (chips, datacenters, power) it owns rather than rents.20%
Expansion pipelineAnnounced capacity, capital, and grid filings that are committed but not yet live.15%
Energy securityPower it has locked in through long-term contracts and owned generation.15%

Help make it better

This index is early and it gets sharper the more people push on it. We are actively looking for input from anyone who knows this terrain: challenge a number you think is wrong, point us at a better or missing source, flag a datacenter or deal we have not captured, or argue for a smarter way to weigh the score. Nothing here is settled.

Open an issue to raise a correction or an idea, or send a pull request against the evidence ledger. Every fact traces to a public source precisely so it can be checked and argued with.
